\subsection{Test Section}\label{test-section}

A normal numbered equation:

\begin{equation}\phantomsection\label{eq-normal}{x = y + z}\end{equation}

A tagged equation (should show ``Condition'' not a number):

\begin{equation*}\tag{\text{Condition}}\label{eq-upstream}
Y \uparrow^{t} E \mid S \setminus \{ Y \}
\end{equation*}

Another tagged equation:

\begin{equation*}\tag{\text{Markov}}\label{eq-markov}
E \perp^t \sigma(\mathcal{C}(E) \setminus S) \mid \sigma(S)
\end{equation*}

A LaTeX-symbol-tagged equation (should show ``★'' not ``\star''):

\begin{equation*}\tag{$\star$}\label{eq-pythag}
x^2 + y^2 = z^2
\end{equation*}

A dollar-wrapped LaTeX tag (should show ``★'' same as bare backslash):

\begin{equation*}\tag{$\star$}\label{eq-cyc-star}
y = x
\end{equation*}

Dollar-wrapped double-star tag:

\begin{equation*}\tag{$\star\star$}\label{eq-dblstar}
\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d}
\end{equation*}

Dollar-wrapped dagger tag:

\begin{equation*}\tag{$\dagger$}\label{eq-dagger}
\alpha + \beta = \gamma
\end{equation*}

Inline-paragraph equation with dollar-wrapped tag. For any variables
\(X_i\) and \(X_j\), consider
\begin{equation*}\tag{$\star$}\label{eq-pairwise}
\frac{P^{t_{X_j}}(X_i)}{P^{t_{X_i}}(X_j)} = \frac{P^t(X_i)}{P^t(X_j)}.
\end{equation*}

Another inline-paragraph equation referencing prior tags. Combined with
\hyperref[eq-pairwise]{$\star$}, this means
\begin{equation*}\tag{$\dagger$}\label{eq-pairwise2}
\frac{Q(X_i)}{Q(X_j)} = \frac{R(X_i)}{R(X_j)}. 
\end{equation*}

Xrefs to \hyperref[eq-pairwise]{$\star$} and
\hyperref[eq-pairwise2]{$\dagger$} in running text.

Another normal numbered equation (should be number 2, not 3):

\begin{equation}\phantomsection\label{eq-second}{a = b + c}\end{equation}
